Sarojini Naidu: The Nightingale of India

About Sarojini Naidu

Sarojini Naidu is known as "one of India's feminist luminaries". Sarojini Naidu Chattopadhyay was born in Hyderabad on 13 February 1879 to Aghorenath Chattopadhyay and Varada Sundari Devi. Her father was a Bengali Brahmin and the principal of Nizam College. Her mother wrote poetry in Bengali. Sarojini Naidu was an Indian political activist and poet. As a poet, Naidu was known as the "Nightingale of India". Edmund Gosse called her "the most accomplished living poet in India" in 1919. She was the former Governor of Uttar Pradesh. A proponent of civil rights, women's emancipation, and anti-imperialism, she played an important role in the Indian independence movement against the British Raj. Naidu's work as a poet earned her the sobriquet Nightingale of India. Naidu died of cardiac arrest at 3:30 p.m. (IST) on 2 March 1949 at the Government House in Lucknow. Her last rites were performed at the Gomati River.

Achievements

  • Naidu's birthday, 13 February, is celebrated as Women's Day to recognise powerful voices of women in India's history.
  • Sarojini Naidu is admired for her work in Indian poetry in addition to her role and commitment to the Indian Nationalist Movement.
  • She was appointed Congress president in 1925.
  • Asteroid 5647 Sarojininaidu, discovered by Eleanor Helin at Palomar Observatory in 1990, was named in her memory.
  • Naidu is memorialized in the Golden Threshold, an off-campus annex of University of Hyderabad named for her first collection of poetry.
  • In 2014, Google India commemorated Naidu's 135th birth anniversary with a Google Doodle.
  • When India achieved its independence, she became the first Governor of the United Provinces in 1947.
  • In 1975, the Government of India Films Division produced a twenty-minute documentary about Naidu's life, "Sarojini Naidu-The Nightingale of India", directed by Bhagwan Das Garga.

  • She inspired women all across the country to step out of their houses and take part in the nation's quest to attain Independence.
  • She was an important face in India's struggle for independence and Salt Satyagraha.
